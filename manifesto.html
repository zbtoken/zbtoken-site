For decades, a quiet war has been fought in the shadows of the internet, a war for privacy, for freedom, for the right to transact without permission. Cypherpunks write code. We don't ask. We don't beg. We build.

On a cold day in January, an anon dropped a 9-page document into a mailing list and disappeared into the void. He proved that money could exist without banks.

Twelve years later, on a quieter day in November, another faceless protocol carved its name into Bitcoin block #710,968, embedding its hash deep into the chain of time. No press release. No ICO. No CEO. Just a timestamp that no one could fake. Zenon was born.

Two protocols. Two revolutions. One unbroken timeline. Bitcoin gave us money without banks. Zenon gave us a network without owners. The orange of one, the green of the other, and now, the alien that carries them both forward.

ZB is the bridge between them. An alien economy where the green of Zenon meets the orange of Bitcoin. A token forged not by a corporation, but by a community of believers, anons, misfits, dreamers, cypherpunks. Those who never asked permission to be free.

We are not building a product. We are building a parallel world, a galaxy where value flows freely between players, creators, and machines. Where every transaction is final. Where every voice is equal. Where no one, no government, no platform, no overlord, can pull the plug.
